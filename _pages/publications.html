Papers

Da Cunha, F. M. S., Araújo, M. M., de Melo, A. C. S., Lira, M. E. D. R., de Oliveira, A. S., Fonseca, F. S., ... & Dos Santos, W. P. (2025, November). Integrating ASTERI-Based EEG Imaging with Deep Learning and Swarm Intelligence for Autism Diagnosis Support. In 2025 IEEE Latin American Conference on Computational Intelligence (LA-CCI) (pp. 1–6). IEEE.
ABS | LINK

The enhancement of instruments for the early diagnosis of Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is of crucial importance for the efficacy of interventions. This study proposes an approach that uses Inverse Distance Weighting (IDW) in the pre-processing of electroencephalographic (EEG) signals and decreasing the number of electrodes in the signal gathering to optimize the biomarkers mapping. Furthermore, deep neural networks (DNN), swarm intelligence, and signal-to-image (STI) conversion techniques are applied to enhance the classification and interpretation of EEG data. The findings have shown that Random Forest classifiers with 4000 and 500 trees achieved the best average results in the reduced and original bases, respectively. In approach 1, the performance was maintained even using only 28.8% of attributes after the particle swarm optimization (PSO). In approach 2, the accuracy was found to be 78.1% and the area under the curve (AUC) was 0.879 with all the attributes. These results demonstrate the effectiveness of pre-processing and attribute reduction strategies, ensuring an efficient solution for the early diagnosis of ASD with operational excellence and lower computational cost
